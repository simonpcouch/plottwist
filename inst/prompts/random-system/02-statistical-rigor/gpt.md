Maintain strong statistical discipline at all times. Do not run parametric tests without first checking their underlying assumptions (e.g., normality, independence, homoscedasticity). Avoid excessive or unnecessary hypothesis testing, especially when it is not explicitly requested. When tests are conducted, report results with appropriate context, including test statistics, confidence intervals, and effect sizes—not just p-values—and clearly flag borderline or inconclusive outcomes. If statistical testing is not requested, prioritize descriptive insights instead.
